Humita Empanadas

Description

Empanadas are little crescent-shaped pastries from Argentina. You can hardly walk down a street in Buenos Aires without passing an empanaderia. They make a great snack - or indeed dinner to share with friends. In this recipe the empanadas are humita flavor.

Humita is a corn-based recipe that is one of the most famous dishes in the gastronomy of northern Argentina, it has a filling of 'guiso andino' (Andean stew) wrapped in a 'chala'. But its consumption does not end in our country, this spectacular food is part of the gastronomy of the so-called Andean area: Chile, Bolivia, Peru, Ecuador and a part of Colombia and Venezuela.

The origin of the word humita is from the Quechua language Humint'a, but in other countries with a similar dish such as Venezuela it is called hallaca, in Bolivia it is called huminta closer to Quechua, in Peru it also bears the name humita and arriving in Central America it is known by the name tamale.

So, we use humita to talk about the preparation in chala, but also to talk about the famous empanada de humita, a recipe that we are going to learn about here.

Corn, a native ingredient of our region, is the star of this recipe, so get ready to make the best creamy corn empanada. Let's get started!

Ingredients

  • 120 grams of yellow corn in canned grains (1 can)
  • (Optional) 4 tablespoons of Mustard
  • 2 onions
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 50 grams of flour
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste
  • 3 oil tablespoons
  • 300 ml of milk
  • 12 covers for empanadas
  • 1 beaten egg (for brushing)

Steps

  1. In a pot with oil, saut√© the onion, the corn and the chopped bell pepper.
  2. Once cooked add the flour and cook for 2 minutes.
  3. Add the milk. Mix well with a whisk. Add the cheese.
  4. Season with mustard and ground pepper and refrigerate.
  5. Once the filling is cold, assemble the empanadas using the traditional repulgue.
  6. Brush with beaten egg and bake.
  7. When they are golden, take them out of the oven and let them cool a little.
